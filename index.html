This Indian Legal Entity Map began as a project to better understand the Indian Judicial System and its structural framework. The initiative emerged from a need to understand the various entities and bodies that influence the functioning of the independent judiciary, including the funding structure, the selection and appointment processes at both central and state levels, and the checks and balances of systems built to enable the Judiciary to act as the custodian and guardian of the Constitution, ensuring that all laws and government actions conform to constitutional principles, interpreting the Constitution, protecting fundamental rights, and exercising judicial review to invalidate any laws or executive acts that violate constitutional provisions; thus, maintaining constitutional supremacy, as a check on the legislature and executive, and safeguarding the rule of law and democratic governance.

This project was initiated following extensive experience managing over 50 cases in India for a financial institution, which raised questions about the efficiency of the justice delivery system. It was also developed after participating in programs that examined the gap between reform initiatives and ground realities. The analysis suggests that judicial reforms face significant challenges, as the incentives for change may not align with broader national progress and the delivery of timely justice.

This documentation is shared in the hope that it may contribute to understanding and inform those working toward meaningful change in the judicial system.
